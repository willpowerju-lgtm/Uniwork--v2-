Supply: Supply growth is now driven by R&D productivity and nodal transitions. For capacity decisions - those are made years in advance,
independent of short-term demand. The company can get the growth without huge amount of incremental capex.
LTA: Long-term agreements, termed "new business models," aim to reduce cyclicality and align business models with customers, moving
beyond traditional agreements that only committed volume. These new business models aim to align the company's and customers'
business models, ensure predictable consumption, and include incentive structures involves a third party holding funds (escrow) to ensure
contract adherence, providing a "soft landing" with cash if a customer must exit. These new models require partners to consume significant
product, grow demand faster than supply, and consume predictably, with customers seeking supply commitments for 2-5 years.
